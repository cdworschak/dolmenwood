## session 11

* absent: Matt
* XP from treasure / player: 0 XP	
* XP from deeds / player: 62 XP
	* entered 4 new hexes (200); learnt about Fairy Roads (250); identified a Leyline - Ywyr (250); confirmed a rumour (The Nag-Lord moves south) partial (400)
 * XP / player: 275 XP (288/302/316)


The group has a friendly encounter with Lace-and-Polkadot, a peddler, who identifies himself as a vassal of Prince Mallowheart. They buy variuous goods off him and tell him about the falls of Naon after he asks for the way.

Tristan buys a handmade elf-chess-board and gets told he is missing some of the pieces to actually play.

Newton gets reminded of the Fairy Roads: Lace-and-Polkdadot tells him he was traversing one of them and was ejected from it, unexpectedly near the Lake, and assumes it was because of Drune magic.

They continue on and stay at an Inn called Mallowhearts Repose. The next day they cross Gawyn's Bridge and see strange black/purple foam forming at the pillars supporting the bridge forming in the water of the river Shub.

Near the Abbey of St. Clewyd they spot a murder of crows that acts strange and they push on to move away from the cursed place with some exhaustion.

The next day they arrive at Swinney Tower and Commander Harrowmore of the order of St.Sedge greets them cautiously. She confirms Joremy's story and the letters he sent. She induces him into the order and gives him a writ that should protect him from legal persecution.

The Commander points him towards the relics and shrine of St. Sedge: find them and bring them back to they may aid in the fight against the Naglord, the "true enemy".

They speak to Jilly, the fey that causes slight chaos in the tower, and Newton asks him a few favours in return for a keg of Ale from Prigwort. Jilly tells them all about the invovlment of the Earl of Yellow with the town as well as the nearby Leyline Ywyr.

In Prigwort they see fortifications being built and they find rest at an Inn.

Gwedin and Backnag go on a two-night bender, but come back unharmed, besides a terrible hang-over.
